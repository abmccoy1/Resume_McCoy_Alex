Summary

I was born into a military family in 1986 in Biloxi, Mississippi. Shortly after my birth my family moved to Michigan where I grew up until I graduated high school in 2004. A few months after my High School graduation, I enlisted in the United States Navy as a Naval Aircrewman. I attended bootcamp in Great Lakes, IL. Upon completion of bootcamp I attended follow on training in Pensacola, FL and Norfolk, VA where I graduated top of my class. When I graduated my training in October 2005, I checked into my first duty station, CVN-72 USS Abraham Lincoln.

I spent two years with the Lincoln before I transferred to VP-30 in November 2007 for training as a Sensor Station One operator in the P-3C Orion model aircraft; graduating top of my class. In Jun 2008, I checked in to Patrol Squadron 45 (VP-45) to perform flight duties as a Sensor Station One Acoustic Operator. During my time at VP-45, I held duties as the Acoustic Track Training Petty Officer, Scheduling Petty Officer, Acoustic Instructor, Acoustic Squadron NATOPS Instructor and Operations Leading Petty Officer. I left VP-45 in May 2011 and transferred to Center for Naval Aviation Training Unit Jacksonville. There I served as an Journeyman Acoustic Instructor until April 2014 when I became a Deparment Leading Petty Officer.

I have been married to my wonderful wife Morgan since October 2012. Together we have three children, two boys ages 8 and 7 and one girl age 2 with a new girl on the way. I enjoy staying active, whether it's playing sports, running, swimming or biking. I coninuously try to find opportunities to expand my education. I feel continuing education is essential in keeping my mind sharp and keeping myself up to date in a rapidly changing industry. I welcome new challenges in any aspect of my life and am capable of solving any problem I am presented.
